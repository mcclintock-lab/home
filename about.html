About the Lab

Our Mission

The McClintock Lab is dedicated to developing innovative web-based tools that advance ocean planning, management, and science. We believe that effective ocean stewardship requires accessible technology that empowers planners, stakeholders, and the public to make informed decisions about our marine resources.

By democratizing access to sophisticated spatial planning and data visualization tools, we're helping communities around the world engage in meaningful, science-based ocean management processes.

What We Do

Our lab specializes in creating user-friendly software solutions for complex marine challenges. We focus on three core areas:

Marine Spatial Planning

We develop tools that enable participatory planning processes, allowing diverse stakeholders to collaborate on ocean use decisions using the best available science and data.

Fisheries Data Collection

We create systems that make it easy for fishermen and managers to collect, visualize, and share fishing data, supporting adaptive management and sustainable fisheries.

Ecological Monitoring

We build platforms that streamline the collection, analysis, and sharing of marine ecological data, making it easier to track ocean health and inform conservation efforts.

Our Approach

We believe that effective ocean management software should be:

  • Accessible: Easy to use for people with varying technical expertise
  • Collaborative: Designed to bring diverse stakeholders together
  • Science-based: Built on sound ecological and spatial principles
  • Open: Transparent and built with open-source technologies when possible
  • Impactful: Focused on real-world applications that lead to better ocean management

Our Impact

Our tools are being used around the globe by government agencies, conservation organizations, fishing communities, and research institutions. From small coastal communities to large regional planning initiatives, our software helps people make better decisions about ocean use and conservation.

SeaSketch alone has been used in marine planning processes spanning millions of square kilometers of ocean, helping to balance conservation goals with sustainable use of marine resources.
